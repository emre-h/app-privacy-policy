# Privacy Policy

**Effective date: June 3, 2026**

This Privacy Policy explains how Derle (the "App") handles information. We built the App to be privacy-first: your code and credentials stay on your device. Please read this policy together with the Terms of Service.

## 1. Our Privacy-First Principle — What We Do NOT Collect

We do not collect, transmit, upload, read, or store any of the following. They never leave your device through the App:

- The source code of any project you create, open, or build in the App.
- The contents of your files, editor buffers, or terminal sessions.
- Your SSH keys or SSH configuration.
- Your Git access tokens, Git credentials, or repository contents.
- Passwords, API keys, or any secrets you enter into your local environment.

All of the above remain locally on your device. We have no server that receives your projects, and we cannot see your code.

## 2. Information Collected Automatically

To keep the App stable and free, we use two Google services that collect a limited amount of technical and usage information:

- **Google Firebase** (Analytics and Crashlytics): usage events (such as which screens are opened), your device model, operating system version, device language, app version, app-instance and advertising identifiers, and — if the App crashes — diagnostic crash reports. This helps us find and fix bugs and understand which features are used.
- **Google AdMob**: your device's advertising identifier and information about your interaction with advertisements (such as ad views and clicks), used to serve and measure ads.

The analytics usage statistics are anonymous signals about how the App is used — for example which project types you create, which features and screens you open, your progress through onboarding, and preference choices such as your selected theme or experience level. We use them only to understand usage and prioritise improvements.

We do not link this technical information to your identity or to the contents of your projects. Some of it — such as device or advertising identifiers — may be treated as personal data under applicable law and is processed by Google as described below.

## 3. Third-Party Services

Firebase and AdMob are provided by Google. Their handling of data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy (policies.google.com/privacy) and, for ads, Google's advertising policies. How Google uses information from apps that use its services is described at policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites. We encourage you to review these. We do not control and are not responsible for the data practices of these providers.

## 4. Advertising and the Advertising ID

The App displays ads through AdMob. Depending on your settings and consent, ads may be personalized or non-personalized. Ads rely on your device's resettable advertising identifier. You can reset this identifier or opt out of ad personalization at any time in your device settings (Settings → Google → Ads). Where required, you will be asked for consent before personalized ads are shown.

## 5. Permissions We Request and Why

- **Internet (network access):** to download the Linux environment, SDKs, packages, and toolchains you choose to install, and to load advertisements and send anonymous analytics/crash reports.
- **Install unknown apps (request to install packages):** so you can install application packages — for example an app you build — directly from the App, with your explicit confirmation each time.
- **Notifications:** to show an ongoing notification for the background service that keeps your terminal sessions and builds alive while the App is in the background.

The App does not request broad storage permissions: your projects are kept in the App's own private storage, and exporting a project writes a .zip to a location you choose through the system file picker, which needs no storage permission.

## 6. How Your Data Is Stored

Your projects, the installed Linux environment, toolchains, and your settings are stored locally in the App's private storage. They remain on your device and under your control.

## 7. Data Retention

Analytics and crash data collected by Firebase, and advertising data collected by AdMob, are retained according to Google's policies. Your local data remains until you delete it or uninstall the App; uninstalling removes the App's local data, including the installed environment and any projects stored in private app storage.

## 8. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. The only parties that receive automatically-collected technical data are Google (through Firebase and AdMob), as described above. We may disclose information if required to do so by law.

## 9. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided such information, please contact us.

## 10. Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal data, or to object to or restrict its processing. Because the technical data described here is collected and held by Google on our behalf, you can exercise many of these choices through your Google and device settings, or by contacting us.

## 11. Security

Credentials you enter — Git access tokens, SSH passwords, and AI provider API keys — are stored encrypted at rest using your device's keystore, and the App's data is excluded from cloud backups. Beyond that we rely on your device's own security model to protect your local data. No method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. Keep your device protected and your credentials safe.

## 12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by an updated effective date. Continued use of the App after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
